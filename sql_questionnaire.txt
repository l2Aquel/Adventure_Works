Phase 1: 
Total Revenue Check: Calculate the total revenue generated in millions for the entire dataset with the currency.
Multi-Dimensional Customer Segmentation Analysis: Write a series of queries that aggregate total revenue and order frequency across Gender, Marital Status, Occupation, and Education Level.
Monthly Revenue Trends: List the total revenue for each month
Top 10 Popular Products: Which 10 products are sold the most and also show the revenue generated by these products.
Sales by Territory: Which country has the highest number of total orders?

Phase 2: 
Customer Loyalty (Churn): Identify "Inactive Customers"—those who haven't placed an order.
Unsold Inventory: Identify all products in the Product_Lookup table that have never been sold.
High-Value Customers: Find the top 10 customers by total spend. Show their full name, email address, orders placed and revenue.
The Quality Control: Generate a report that identifies products that have both high sales volume (over 10,000 units) and significant return volume (over 5,000 units).
Weekend vs. Weekday: Analyze total revenue broken down by the Day of the Week.

Phase 3: 
Income Bracket Performance: Fragment customers into 'Low' (<$5k), 'Medium' ($5k-$10k), and 'High' (>$10k) income brackets and find the total revenue generated by each bracket.
Month-over-Month Growth: Calculate the monthly revenue and the percentage change compared to the previous month.
Top 3 Customers per Occupation: Find the top 3 spending customers within each occupation category.
Rolling 7-Day Revenue: Calculate a 7-day rolling average of revenue to smooth out daily sales spikes.
Returning Customers: Write a query that identifies every instance where a customer had a "silent gap" of 730 days (2 years) or more between any two consecutive orders.
